How Does Tick Trading Work?

Tick trading works on the concept of tick size – the smallest price change of a security. Every security has a predefined tick size which means how much the price can move. For example if the tick size of a stock is ₹0.05 then the price can only move in multiples of ₹0.05. This ensures prices move in a controlled and measurable way, maintaining order and market transparency. For example, let’s say Adani Enterprises is trading at ₹3,183.00 with a tick size of ₹0.05. The next valid price can only be ₹3,183.05 (up) or ₹3,182.95 (down). Similarly, Aegis Logistics is trading at ₹758.40 and will move to ₹758.45 or ₹758.35 based on the same tick size. By controlling price movement increments, tick trading makes decision-making easier for traders and ensures price movements are structured and predictable. This is very useful in tick chart trading, where traders analyze real-time price movements to identify trends, breakouts or reversals. The fixed tick size helps traders to anticipate price movements more accurately in high-volume high-speed markets like stocks, futures and options.
